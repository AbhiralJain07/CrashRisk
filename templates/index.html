From raw data to
actionable risk intelligence.

// 03 · Key Risk Features

Eleven factors. One verdict.

✈️
Aircraft Age
Older airframes accumulate mechanical fatigue. Combined with deferred maintenance, age is a critical compounding multiplier.
Base Weight: 14%
🔧
Maintenance Recency
Days since the last maintenance check. The top single predictor — outstanding airworthiness directives heavily elevate risk scores.
Base Weight: 16%
👨‍✈️
Pilot Experience
Total flight hours weighted against route and aircraft complexity. Logarithmic scaling reflects the diminishing marginal return of experience.
Base Weight: 18%
📅
Pilot Recency
Days since the pilot's last flight. Currency of type-specific flight is a stronger predictor than total logged hours alone.
Base Weight: 10%
🌩️
Weather Severity
Composite IMC score from precipitation, crosswinds, and ceiling conditions. A peak-risk trigger when crossing critical thresholds.
Base Weight: 15%
👁️
Visibility
Statute miles of reported visibility. Below 3 miles, approach and landing incident rates climb exponentially in the training data.
Base Weight: 8%
💨
Turbulence
Forecast turbulence severity. A significant factor in structural stress events, crew incapacitation, and loss-of-control incidents.
Base Weight: 8%
🛫
Airport Complexity
Composite index: runway configuration, traffic density, ILS category, terrain proximity, and approach complexity at the destination.
Base Weight: 6%
🌙
Time + Season
Night operations and winter seasons carry validated historical risk multipliers across 30+ years of global incident data.
Base Weight: 5%

// 05 · Classification System

Four tiers. Clear action protocols.

✅
LOW
Flight conditions within normal safety parameters. Standard pre-flight protocols apply. No additional review required.
~37% of scenarios
⚠️
MODERATE
Elevated risk factors detected. Enhanced crew briefing, additional monitoring, and operational contingencies recommended.
~51% of scenarios
🔴
HIGH
Multiple risk factors converging. Operational review required. Consider delay, alternate routing, or crew augmentation.
~9% of scenarios
🚨
CRITICAL
Critical risk level. Immediate safety review by senior operations required before any flight operations proceed.
~3% of scenarios
